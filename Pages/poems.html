Poems

I started writing when I was in 7th grade as a way to cope with then-undiagnosed mental illnesses. By 9th grade, I had filled multiple notebooks, and by 10th I started reciting poems to cope with inpatient. Eventually, I started crafting my own, using it as a translator for my emotions. As of 2023, I have 40+ poems written in the hopes of publishing a book someday. Here is a mix of poems I've written. My oldest would be A Life to my latest poem The Five W's. Comparing the them, you can read my abilities over time getting better as I hone my skills as a poet and storyteller. At the bottom of the page are hotline numbers for anyone who is suffering from depression or abuse or is thinking about running away.

10/14/2023 •

Mutually Ensure Destruction

I wrote this poem a few days after October 7th and reading the news. However, what pained me more than that day was reading the history of Isreal and the subsequent apartide that's existed there for decades. Seeing images of Gaza decimated into rubble, turning into an unliveable warzone. I read about the leveling of civilian infrastructure and watched unwavering support for a country committing genocide. This poem is to express my pain for the Palestinian people.

The Five W's

This poem follows me asking myself the five w's. Who, What, When, Where and Why. The constant theme is me questioning who is to blame for my abuse as a child. As well as me expressing the anger I hold towards my parents. I do love them dearly, but there are things I have yet to forgive them over.
